EDA Learning Summary
For this assignment, I performed Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) on the MBA Supply Chain dataset. 
Through this dataset, I was able to understand how different factors like product type, transportation mode,
supplier performance, and costs are connected within a supply chain system. 
It helped me see how real-world business data is structured and how multiple variables together 
influence overall efficiency and outcomes.
At the beginning, I loaded the dataset using pandas and explored it using 
functions like head(), info(), and describe(). This gave me a basic idea of the dataset structure, 
types of data, and summary statistics. I also checked for missing values and duplicate records,
which is an important step before doing any analysis. Fortunately, the dataset was mostly clean,
but understanding how to handle such issues was useful.
One of the challenges I faced was deciding which visualizations to use and how to interpret them properly. 
Initially, I created graphs without clear understanding, but gradually I learned how histograms 
help in understanding distribution, countplots help compare categories, and scatter plots show relationships between variables.
The correlation heatmap was especially helpful in identifying strong and weak relationships between numerical features.
Using seaborn and matplotlib, I created multiple visualizations like histograms, bar plots, boxplots,
and scatter plots. Among these, the heatmap and boxplots were the most useful because they clearly 
showed trends and outliers in the data.
From the analysis, I observed that certain transportation modes had higher costs, and some suppliers 
performed better consistently. I also noticed patterns in product demand and pricing that could affect 
decision-making in supply chain management.
Overall, this assignment helped me gain practical experience with Python libraries like
pandas, numpy, seaborn, and matplotlib. More importantly, I understood how EDA helps in extracting
meaningful insights from raw data and supports better decision-making.
